\begin{abstract}
\noindent
Autonomous agents are increasingly configured by \emph{skills}: natural-language
specifications that an LLM drafts from a user's stated intent and then executes,
often across several cooperating agents. Such drafts fail expensively at run time
on plans that were impossible from the start --- a step that invokes a tool the
agent does not have, a goal conjunct nothing can establish, a budget no run can
meet, or a multi-agent handoff that deadlocks. We present a \emph{skill compiler}
that decides, before execution and without an LLM judge, whether a skill's
\emph{goal is achievable at all}. The compiler fuses two mature disciplines not
previously combined for this purpose: capability-guarded reachability from
automated planning, and projectability and deadlock-freedom from multiparty
session types, decided over an intent-synthesized, goal-marked global type. The
LLM that compacts prose into a formal pack of preconditions, effects, and goals
is treated as \emph{untrusted}; the checker is a mechanically verified core. We
prove in Coq that the checker is \emph{sound for refutation} --- a verdict of
\emph{impossible} is never wrong --- while being deliberately \emph{incomplete}
for achievability, with the residue confined to two precisely characterized edges
(payload faithfulness and intent fidelity) handed respectively to a runtime
monitor and a human checkpoint. We give the syntax, an operational semantics, the
typing and achievability judgments with inference rules, and the metatheory:
refutation soundness, tolerance soundness, capability monotonicity, decidability
within a static-topology fragment, and undecidability once agents may spawn
participants at run time. An implementation and a corpus spanning the canonical
agent failure modes confirm the theory: zero false impossibilities, and every
false achievability an instance of the two deferred edges. This extended
version adds four implementation-driven strengthenings with proofs --- a
protocol-independent \emph{establisher-closure} refutation that survives the
undecidable autonomous fragment, \emph{observed choice} for
conversation-embedded coordination, an \emph{adversarial} (must-)achievability
mode whose refutations inherit soundness compositionally, and
counterexample-guided compaction repair --- and an evaluation on a corpus of
real, deployed skills: every deployed skill checks achievable in its home
capability context, every seeded semantic fault is refuted with the missing
tool or dead goal conjunct named.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

A modern agent is rarely a single model call. It is a \emph{skill}: a
specification --- increasingly authored in natural language and drafted by an LLM
from a user's stated goal --- that names tools, describes a plan, and coordinates
one or more agents toward an outcome. The appeal is that a non-expert can say
\emph{what} they want and have the system assemble the \emph{how}. The hazard is
that the assembled how is frequently \emph{impossible}, and nothing notices until
execution has already spent tokens, taken actions, and, in the multi-agent case,
deadlocked halfway.

The failures are structural and recurring. A plan \emph{books a flight and emails
a confirmation} although no email tool is in scope (a hallucinated capability). A
plan pursues \emph{a flight under \$500} when the only booking tool returns
premium fares (an unsatisfiable refinement). A planner waits for a worker's result
while the worker waits for the planner's answer, each listening for a message the
other never sends (a coordination deadlock). In every case the goal was unreachable
\emph{a priori}, and a cheap static check could have said so.

This paper asks a deliberately narrow question and answers it with a guarantee:
\textbf{given an LLM-drafted skill, can its goal be achieved at all?} We are not
(here) verifying that every step is safe, nor that the agent behaves correctly on
every input. We decide \emph{achievability}, tolerantly --- small details, detours,
and payload variation must not raise a false alarm --- and \emph{soundly for
refutation}: when the compiler says a goal is impossible, it is right.

\subsection{The idea, and the trust boundary}

The architecture has two halves separated by a trust boundary. An LLM performs
\emph{compaction}: it reads the prose skill and distills it into a formal
\emph{pack} --- capabilities annotated with preconditions and effects, a
goal-marked global protocol, and an initial state. This step is \emph{untrusted};
the LLM may misread the prose. A deterministic \emph{checker} then decides
achievability over the pack, and the checker is mechanically proved sound. The
division is what makes the system honest: a faulty compaction can only ever cause
a false \emph{achievable} (caught downstream), never a false \emph{impossible}
about the pack it received.

Crucially, the semantic gap between \emph{what the user meant} and \emph{what was
formalized} is not eliminated --- it is \emph{relocated and concentrated} into one
inspectable checkpoint at compaction time, rather than scattered, unobserved,
across every run. That relocation is the central design move.

\subsection{Contributions}
\begin{enumerate}\setlength{\itemsep}{2pt}
\item \textbf{A goal-achievability type discipline} (\S3--\S5) that fuses
capability-guarded reachability (planning) with projectability and
deadlock-freedom (multiparty session types), decided over an intent-synthesized,
goal-marked global type. To our knowledge this fusion, aimed at LLM-drafted agent
skills, is new.
\item \textbf{A mechanized soundness core} (\S6) in Coq: refutation soundness
(a verdict of impossible is never wrong), tolerance soundness (coarsening the
abstraction preserves refutation), and capability monotonicity (more tools never
make a goal impossible) --- all axiom-free.
\item \textbf{A decidability boundary result} (\S6.4): achievability is decidable
in a static-topology fragment and undecidable once agents may spawn participants
at run time. Autonomy is exactly what crosses the line.
\item \textbf{An implementation and evaluation} (\S8--\S9): a checker deciding
all four premises of the judgment (capability soundness, projection with merge,
Gay--Hole conformance, may-reachability), an LLM-compaction front-end with a
schema gate, and a corpus spanning the canonical failure modes. Results match
the theory: zero false impossibilities; every false achievability is one of the
two deferred residues.
\item \textbf{Implementation-driven strengthenings, with proofs} (\S7):
the establisher-closure certificate (protocol-independent refutation, valid even
under dynamic spawning), observed choice (conversation-embedded selection is an
implicit broadcast; unobserved choice is a ${\ge}3$-party asynchronous
phenomenon), adversarial must-achievability (refutation soundness inherited
compositionally from Theorem~\ref{thm:ref}), and counterexample-guided
compaction repair at the trust boundary.
\item \textbf{Validation on real skills} (\S9): on Anthropic's public skills
corpus, the compiler judges every deployed skill achievable in its home
capability context (no false alarms), refutes the same skills under a
capability-poorer runtime with the exact missing tool and source line, and
catches every seeded semantic fault (dropped capability, stripped goal
establisher) naming the wound.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Overview by Example}

Consider a one-agent skill whose goal is \emph{a flight is booked and a
confirmation is sent}. The author writes prose; the LLM compacts it. Two
compactions differ only in whether an email tool is in scope.
\[
\gphi \;=\; \mathsf{booked} \,\wedge\, \mathsf{confirmation\_sent}
\]
\begin{center}\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
\textbf{variant A (no email tool)} & \textbf{variant B} \\
$\mathsf{search}: \Add\,\{\mathsf{searched}\}$ & $\dots$ A, plus: \\
$\mathsf{filter}: \pre\ \mathsf{searched};\ \Add\,\{\mathsf{filtered}\}$ & $\mathsf{email}: \pre\ \mathsf{booked};$ \\
$\mathsf{book}: \pre\ \mathsf{filtered};\ \Add\,\{\mathsf{booked}\}$ & $\qquad\Add\,\{\mathsf{confirmation\_sent}\}$
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Under STRIPS \emph{frame semantics} --- a predicate is false unless an effect
makes it true --- variant A has no action establishing
$\mathsf{confirmation\_sent}$, so no reachable state satisfies the goal: the
checker returns \emph{impossible} and points at the missing establisher. Variant B
reaches the goal along $\mathsf{search}\cdot\mathsf{filter}\cdot\mathsf{book}\cdot
\mathsf{email}$, returned as a witness. The same example is mechanized in \S6 (the
concrete instance \id{FlightInstance}): variant A is \emph{provably} unachievable,
yet a booking is still reachable --- the refutation is about the missing tool, not
a dead protocol.

Tolerance is essential, and is where a naive reachability check would misfire.
Extra status messages, a clarifying detour, or payload variation must not refute
the goal. We obtain this from three orthogonal relaxations made precise in
\S4--\S5: \emph{existential may-reachability} (a single good path suffices),
\emph{session subtyping} (interface slack in the safe direction), and
\emph{goal-relevant abstraction} (payload detail collapsed before checking).

\section{Syntax}

We fix disjoint sets of predicates $p\in\Pred$ (boolean), numeric variables
$x\in\Var$, roles $\mathsf{p},\mathsf{q}\in\mathcal R$, capability names
$a\in\CapS$, and message labels $\ell\in\Lab$.

\subsection{State logic}
\[
\begin{array}{rcl@{\qquad}l}
e &::=& x \mid n \mid e+e \mid e-e \mid c\cdot e & (c,n\in\mathbb Z)\\[1pt]
\varphi &::=& p \mid \top \mid \bot \mid \neg\varphi \mid \varphi\wedge\varphi
  \mid \varphi\vee\varphi \mid e\bowtie e & \bowtie\,\in\{<,\le,=,\ge,>,\ne\}
\end{array}
\]
The state logic $\mathcal L$ is quantifier-free linear integer arithmetic with
uninterpreted boolean predicates --- a decidable fragment for which entailment
$\vDash$ and satisfiability are discharged by an SMT solver. The
implementation's expression grammar accepts general products $e\cdot e$;
decidability (Theorem~\ref{thm:dec}) is claimed for linear use only, and the
checker resolves any solver \emph{unknown} conservatively as satisfiable, so
outside the linear fragment it can lose refutations but never soundness.

\subsection{Worlds and capabilities}
A \emph{world} $W=\langle B,N\rangle$ valuates predicates $B:\Pred\to\mathbb B$ and
numeric variables $N:\Var\to\mathbb Z$. A \emph{capability context} $\Gamma$ maps
each available tool $a$ to a guarded effect:
\[
\Gamma(a)=\langle \pre_a,\eff_a\rangle,\quad \pre_a\in\mathcal L,\qquad
\eff_a=\langle \Add_a\subseteq\Pred,\ \Del_a\subseteq\Pred,\
\Asg_a:\Var\rightharpoonup e,\ \Nd_a:\Var\rightharpoonup\mathcal L\rangle
\]
\[
a\notin\dom(\Gamma)\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad\text{the agent does \emph{not}
possess tool }a\quad(\text{no hallucinated tools}).
\]
$\Add/\Del$ are the STRIPS add- and delete-lists; $\Asg$ gives deterministic
numeric updates $x:=e$; $\Nd$ gives \emph{nondeterministic} updates $x:=\ast$
constrained by a formula over the new value (used to model post-conditions such as
\emph{``books a fare under 500''}).

\subsection{Global types, local types, skills}
A \emph{goal-marked global type} $G$ describes the whole choreography;
\emph{local types} $T$ are its per-role projections; a \emph{skill} $S_{\mathsf p}$
is the behaviour the author declares for role $\mathsf p$.
\[
\begin{array}{rcll}
G &::=& \mathsf{p}{\to}\mathsf{q}:\ell\langle\hat\alpha\rangle.\,G
  & \text{message ($\mathsf p$ sends $\ell$ to $\mathsf q$, payload abstracted)}\\
  &\mid& \mathsf{p}\triangleright\{\ell_i:G_i\}_{i\in I} & \text{choice ($\mathsf p$ selects a branch)}\\
  &\mid& a@\mathsf{p}.\,G & \text{action (role $\mathsf p$ invokes capability $a$)}\\
  &\mid& \checkmark\varphi.\,G & \text{goal marker (the protocol claims $\varphi$ holds)}\\
  &\mid& \mathsf{spawn}\ \mathsf{q}.\,G & \text{dynamic participant (outside the decidable fragment)}\\
  &\mid& \mu X.\,G \mid X \mid \mathbf{end} & \\[3pt]
T &::=& \mathsf{q}!\ell\langle\hat\alpha\rangle.T \mid
  \mathsf{q}?\ell\langle\hat\alpha\rangle.T \mid
  \oplus\{\ell_i:T_i\} \mid \&\{\ell_i:T_i\} \\
  & & \mid\ a.T \mid
  \checkmark\varphi.T \mid \mu X.T \mid X \mid \mathbf{end}\\[3pt]
S_{\mathsf p} &::=& \text{(same grammar as $T$)} & \text{the declared behaviour of role $\mathsf p$}
\end{array}
\]
The abstraction $\alpha$ maps concrete messages to goal-relevant tokens
$\hat\alpha$; it is the tolerance dial of \S4.4. Only $a@\mathsf p$ steps change
the world; messages carry coordination information and are abstracted for
achievability. Well-formedness (enforced by the implementation's schema gate)
requires recursion variables $X$ to occur only in \emph{tail} position under
uniquely-named binders --- the fragment of Theorem~\ref{thm:dec} --- and
$\mathsf{spawn}$ marks the protocol as outside that fragment
(Theorem~\ref{thm:undec}); choices may carry the annotations
$\triangleright^{\mathrm{obs}}$ (\S4.3) and $\triangleright^{\mathrm{ext}}$
(\S7.2).

\section{Operational Semantics}

\subsection{World transitions}
A capability fires when its guard holds, producing a successor world. Because
$\Nd$ is nondeterministic, $\langle W\rangle\,a\,\langle W'\rangle$ may relate one
world to many.
\begin{mathpar}
\inferrule[World-Act]{W\vDash\pre_a \\ W'\in\apply(\eff_a,W)}
  {\langle W\rangle\;a\;\langle W'\rangle}
\end{mathpar}
\[
\apply(\eff_a,\langle B,N\rangle)=\langle B',N'\rangle,\qquad
B'=(B\cup\Add_a)\setminus\Del_a
\]
\[
N'(x)=\begin{cases}
\llbracket\Asg_a(x)\rrbracket_N & \text{if } x\in\dom(\Asg_a)\\
v \text{ with } \langle B,N[x{\mapsto}v]\rangle\vDash\Nd_a(x) & \text{if } x\in\dom(\Nd_a)\\
N(x) & \text{otherwise (frame)}
\end{cases}
\]

\subsection{Configurations and may-reachability}
A \emph{configuration} is a pair $(G,W)$. The transition $\reach$ advances
control, firing world transitions at actions and branching at choices; $\reachs$
is its reflexive-transitive closure.
\begin{mathpar}
\inferrule[R-Act]{\langle W\rangle\,a\,\langle W'\rangle}{(a@\mathsf p.\,G,\,W)\reach(G,\,W')}
\and
\inferrule[R-Msg]{ }{(\mathsf p{\to}\mathsf q{:}\ell\langle\hat\alpha\rangle.G,\,W)\reach(G,\,W)}
\and
\inferrule[R-Sel]{k\in I}{(\mathsf p\triangleright\{\ell_i:G_i\}_{i\in I},\,W)\reach(G_k,\,W)}
\and
\inferrule[R-Rec]{ }{(\mu X.G,\,W)\reach(G[\mu X.G/X],\,W)}
\and
\inferrule[R-Chk]{ }{(\checkmark\varphi.G,\,W)\reach(G,\,W)}
\end{mathpar}
\textsc{R-Chk} makes goal markers \emph{observation points}, not blocking
assertions: a run may pass an unsatisfied marker and satisfy the goal later
(and the checker does exactly this). A configuration is \emph{goal-satisfying}
when control is at a goal marker or terminus and the world entails the goal:
\[
\goal(\checkmark\varphi.G,W)\iff W\vDash\varphi,\qquad
\goal(\mathbf{end},W)\iff W\vDash\gphi.
\]
\[
\Gamma;G\achbox\gphi\iff
\exists W_0\vDash\mathit{init}.\ \exists(G',W').\ (G,W_0)\reachs(G',W')\ \wedge\ \goal(G',W').
\]
The diamond is \emph{existential}: a single witnessing run suffices. This is the
formal source of detour-tolerance. The initial condition $\mathit{init}$ has
two parts, mirroring the world: a set of predicates true at the start (the
frame closes it --- everything unlisted is false, so $B_0$ is \emph{fixed})
and a set of $\mathcal L$-constraints on the numeric part, over which the
existential genuinely ranges.

\subsection{Projection and realizability}
Projection $G\restr{\mathsf p}$ extracts role $\mathsf p$'s local type, using the
merge $\sqcap$ on continuations of a choice in which $\mathsf p$ is not the
selector. It is \emph{partial}: undefined exactly when a non-selecting role cannot
tell which branch was taken (the \emph{unobserved-choice} condition).
\begin{mathpar}
\inferrule[Proj-Sel]{G_i\restr{\mathsf p}=T_i\ (i\in I)}
  {(\mathsf p\triangleright\{\ell_i:G_i\})\restr{\mathsf p}=\oplus\{\ell_i:T_i\}}
\and
\inferrule[Proj-Brn]{G_i\restr{\mathsf q}=\mathsf r?\ell'_i.\,T_i\ (i\in I) \\ \text{the }\ell'_i\text{ pairwise distinct, common sender }\mathsf r}
  {(\mathsf p\triangleright\{\ell_i:G_i\})\restr{\mathsf q}=\&\{\ell'_i:T_i\}}
\and
\inferrule[Proj-Mrg]{G_i\restr{\mathsf q}=T_i\ (i\in I) \\ T=\sqcap_{i}\,T_i \text{ defined}}
  {(\mathsf p\triangleright\{\ell_i:G_i\})\restr{\mathsf q}=T}
\end{mathpar}
$G$ is \emph{realizable}, $G\Downarrow\{T_{\mathsf p}\}$, when $G\restr{\mathsf p}$
is defined for every role. Non-realizability is the static signature of a
deadlocking handoff. \textsc{Proj-Brn} as implemented is slightly more
liberal than the textbook rule: the distinguishing receive need not come from
the selector $\mathsf p$ --- any \emph{common} informant $\mathsf r$ whose
labels separate the branches suffices (the knowledge of the choice may be
relayed); it must, however, be the \emph{first} thing $\mathsf q$ does in
each branch.

\paragraph{Observed choice.} Real skills are frequently \emph{one
conversation}: an assistant and its user in a chat, or an agent and a business
on a phone call. On such a medium the outcome of a choice is announced by the
medium itself --- every participant hears which way the call went. We therefore
allow a choice to be annotated \emph{observed},
$\mathsf p\triangleright^{\mathrm{obs}}\{\ell_i:G_i\}$, projecting for a
non-selector as an external choice on the selection labels directly:
\begin{mathpar}
\inferrule[Proj-Obs]{G_i\restr{\mathsf q}=T_i\ (i\in I) \\ \mathsf q\ne\mathsf p}
  {(\mathsf p\triangleright^{\mathrm{obs}}\{\ell_i:G_i\})\restr{\mathsf q}=\&\{\ell_i:T_i\}}
\end{mathpar}
(when all $T_i$ coincide the merge applies as usual and the annotation is
inert). Section~\ref{sec:obs} proves this sound by desugaring into explicit
broadcasts and observes that the unobserved-choice condition is intrinsically a
${\ge}3$-party \emph{asynchronous} phenomenon.

\subsection{Subtyping and the tolerance dial}
We use Gay--Hole branching subtyping $\le$: a skill may offer \emph{more} external
(receive) choices and \emph{fewer} internal (send) choices than its contract and
remain safe.
\begin{mathpar}
\inferrule[Sub-Ext]{I\supseteq J \\ T_i\le T'_i\ (i\in J)}
  {\&\{\ell_i:T_i\}_{i\in I}\ \le\ \&\{\ell_j:T'_j\}_{j\in J}}
\and
\inferrule[Sub-Int]{I\subseteq J \\ T_i\le T'_i\ (i\in I)}
  {\oplus\{\ell_i:T_i\}_{i\in I}\ \le\ \oplus\{\ell_j:T'_j\}_{j\in J}}
\end{mathpar}
Tolerance has three independent knobs: the existential $\Diamond$ (\S4.2),
subtyping $\le$ (here), and the granularity of $\alpha$. ``Flexible, tolerant of
small details'' is the profile $\langle$coarse $\alpha$, $\Diamond$, linear
$\mathcal L\rangle$; a later safety layer flips the same machinery to the universal
$\Box$ over permission predicates.

\section{The Achievability Type System}

Achievability factors into four premises, each separately decidable, combined by
the top rule \textsc{Ach}.

\subsection{Capability and goal typing}
\begin{mathpar}
\inferrule[T-Act]{\Gamma(a)=\langle\pre,\eff\rangle \\ \varphi\vDash\pre}
  {\Gamma\vdash\{\varphi\}\,a\,\{\seither(\varphi,\eff)\}}
\and
\inferrule[T-Miss]{a\notin\dom(\Gamma)}
  {\Gamma\vdash a\ \triangleright\ \text{\ding{55}}\ (\textsc{Missing\_Capability})}
\end{mathpar}
$\seither(\varphi,\eff)$ is the strongest postcondition of the STRIPS effect.
\textsc{T-Miss} fires on hallucinated planning: the plan names a tool the context
does not grant, and achievability is refuted without any search.

\subsection{The achievability judgment}
\begin{mathpar}
\inferrule[Ach]{\Gamma\supseteq\caps(G) \\ G\Downarrow\{T_{\mathsf p}\} \\
  \forall\mathsf p.\ \Gamma\vdash S_{\mathsf p}\le G\restr{\mathsf p} \\
  \Gamma;G\achbox\gphi}
  {\Gamma\vdash\{S_{\mathsf p}\}_{\mathsf p}\ :\ G\ \triangleright\ \Diamond\gphi}
\end{mathpar}
Read top-to-bottom: no hallucinated tools; the global type is realizable (no
deadlocking handoff); every declared skill conforms to its projected contract up
to subtyping; and the contract can reach the goal. The judgment is decided on $G$,
and subtyping \emph{transports} the verdict to the actual skills --- legitimate by
Theorem~\ref{thm:tol}. Declared behaviours are optional: a role without an
$S_{\mathsf p}$ takes its projection $G\restr{\mathsf p}$ as its definition,
making the conformance premise vacuous for it. The implementation decides the
premises in a fixed order chosen so that the cheap, topology-independent
refutations fire first: capability soundness, then the establisher-closure
certificate (\S\ref{sec:closure}), then the fragment boundary
($\mathsf{spawn}\Rightarrow$ \textsc{Unknown}), then projection, conformance,
and finally reachability. Each refutation names its premise:
\textsc{Missing\_Capability}, \textsc{Goal\_Unsat},
\textsc{Blocked\_Guard}, \textsc{Non\_Projectable},
\textsc{Non\_Conformant}, or the non-verdict \textsc{Unknown}
(\textsc{Dynamic\_Topology}).

\subsection{Reachability rules}
The diamond is derived by the following inductive system, realized by the checker
as a finite forward search.
\begin{mathpar}
\inferrule[Reach-Goal]{W\vDash\gphi}{\Gamma;(G,W)\achbox\gphi}
\and
\inferrule[Reach-Step]{(G,W)\reach(G',W') \\ \Gamma;(G',W')\achbox\gphi}{\Gamma;(G,W)\achbox\gphi}
\and
\inferrule[Reach-Or]{\exists k\in I.\ \Gamma;(G_k,W)\achbox\gphi}
  {\Gamma;(\mathsf p\triangleright\{\ell_i:G_i\},W)\achbox\gphi}
\end{mathpar}

\section{Metatheory}

We separate what is \emph{mechanized in Coq} (Theorems~\ref{thm:ref}--\ref{thm:cap}
and the concrete instance, all axiom-free) from what is \emph{proved on paper}
(decidability and the undecidability boundary).

\subsection{Soundness of the abstraction}
Let $\mathcal A$ be the abstract transition system the checker explores and
$\mathit{abs}:W\to\mathcal A$ the abstraction. We require two simulation conditions:
\[
\textbf{(step-sim)}\ \ W\reach W'\Rightarrow \mathit{abs}(W)\,\hat\reach\,\mathit{abs}(W'),
\qquad
\textbf{(goal-sim)}\ \ \goal(W)\Rightarrow\hat\goal(\mathit{abs}(W)).
\]
\begin{lemma}[Transport]\label{lem:transport}
If $(G,W_0)\reachs(G',W)$ then $\mathit{abs}(W_0)\,\hat\reach^{*}\,\mathit{abs}(W)$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Induction on the closure; \textsc{(step-sim)} discharges the step case. Mechanized
as \id{reach\_abs}.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Refutation soundness, tolerance, monotonicity}
\begin{theorem}[Refutation soundness]\label{thm:ref}
If the abstract system has no reachable goal state from $\mathit{abs}(W_0)$, then
no concrete run from $W_0$ reaches $\gphi$. Hence a verdict of \emph{impossible} is
never wrong.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Contrapositive of Lemma~\ref{lem:transport} composed with \textsc{(goal-sim)}.
Mechanized, axiom-free:
\end{proof}
\begin{lstlisting}[style=coq]
Theorem refutation_sound : forall s0,
  (~ exists a, reach astep (abs s0) a /\ agoal a) ->
  (~ exists w, reach cstep s0 w /\ cgoal w).
(* Print Assumptions refutation_sound.  =>  Closed under the global context *)
\end{lstlisting}

\begin{theorem}[Tolerance soundness]\label{thm:tol}
Let $\hat\reach_1\subseteq\hat\reach_2$ be two abstract step relations over the
same states (the coarser $\hat\reach_2$ has \emph{more} edges), with the same
goal predicate. If $\hat\reach_2$ cannot reach a goal state from
$\mathit{abs}(W_0)$, neither can $\hat\reach_1$; composed with
Theorem~\ref{thm:ref}, a refutation computed on any coarser over-approximation
transfers to the concrete system. Hence coarsening $\alpha$ to tolerate more
detail never produces a false refutation. (Coq: \id{tolerance\_sound}.)
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Reachability is monotone in the step relation: if $R_1\subseteq R_2$ then
$\mathrm{reach}(R_1)\subseteq\mathrm{reach}(R_2)$, by induction on the
reflexive--transitive closure --- the reflexive case is immediate, and a step
$u\,R_1\,v$ is also a step $u\,R_2\,v$ (mechanized as \id{reach\_mono}).
Contraposing: no goal state reachable in $R_2$ implies none reachable in $R_1$.
Taking $R_1$ to be the abstract image of the concrete system guaranteed by
\textsc{(step-sim)} and $R_2$ the coarser exploration relation, composing with
Lemma~\ref{lem:transport} and \textsc{(goal-sim)} yields the concrete claim
exactly as in Theorem~\ref{thm:ref}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Capability monotonicity]\label{thm:cap}
Let $\Gamma\subseteq\Gamma'$ agree on their common domain. If some run over
$\Gamma$ reaches $\gphi$, then some run over $\Gamma'$ reaches $\gphi$.
Granting tools never makes an achievable goal impossible. (Coq:
\id{cap\_monotone}.)
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Every transition of the $\Gamma$-system is a transition of the
$\Gamma'$-system: \textsc{R-Msg}, \textsc{R-Sel}, \textsc{R-Rec} do not
consult $\Gamma$, and a \textsc{World-Act} step through $a\in\dom(\Gamma)$
uses $\Gamma(a)=\Gamma'(a)$, so the same successor world is produced. Thus
$\reach_\Gamma\subseteq\reach_{\Gamma'}$, and the claim is
\id{reach\_mono} applied to the witnessing run. At the judgment level the
other premises are unaffected: projection and conformance do not mention
$\Gamma$, and $\Gamma\supseteq\caps(G)$ only becomes easier. Note the
asymmetry: \emph{removing} a capability can (and should) flip a verdict ---
that is the refutation direction the mutation tests of \S\ref{sec:realeval}
exercise.
\end{proof}

\noindent\textbf{Concrete instance.} The flight skill of \S2, variant A, is
mechanized as \id{FlightInstance}. We prove \id{flight\_refuted} (no run reaches
$\mathsf{booked}\wedge\mathsf{confirmation\_sent}$, since no step touches
$\mathsf{conf}$) and \id{booking\_reachable} (a booking is still reachable). Both
are axiom-free, witnessing non-vacuity of Theorem~\ref{thm:ref}.

\subsection{Incompleteness, by design}
\begin{proposition}[Incompleteness]\label{prop:inc}
$\Gamma;G\achbox\gphi$ may hold while no concrete run reaches $\gphi$: the
witnessing abstract path can be spurious when $\alpha$ collapses a distinction
that mattered. The system is \emph{sound for \ding{55} and incomplete for
\ding{51}}.
\end{proposition}
This is the price of decidable, tolerant over-approximation; tightening $\alpha$
trades tolerance for precision. The residue is confined to two edges, motivating
the layered architecture:
\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{1pt}
\item \textbf{Payload faithfulness} (bottom edge): a tool's declared
post-condition (``filters to fares $<500$'') may be false --- a runtime obligation
for a deterministic monitor (Layer~C), not a static one.
\item \textbf{Intent fidelity} (top edge): the formalized goal may not capture what
the user meant --- irreducibly semantic, surfaced at the single compaction
checkpoint for human review.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Decidability and the autonomy boundary}
\begin{theorem}[Decidability]\label{thm:dec}
In the fragment with finitely many roles (static topology), tail-recursive $G$,
decidable state logic $\mathcal L$, and $\mathcal L$-definable effects,
$\Gamma;G\achbox\gphi$ is decidable.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
We exhibit the decision procedure the implementation realizes and prove it
terminating and sound.

\emph{Symbolic configurations.} A symbolic configuration is a triple
$\sigma=(c,P,\phi)$: a control point $c$ in the protocol (a position in the
finite step tree; by tail-recursion every $\mu X$ body returns control to a
fixed loop head, so the set $C$ of control points is finite), a finite set
$P\subseteq\Pred$ of predicates currently true (frame: all others false), and
a path constraint $\phi\in\mathcal L$ over SSA-indexed numeric variables.
$\sigma$ over-approximates the set of worlds
$\{\langle B,N\rangle : B=P,\ N\vDash\exists\text{-closure of }\phi\}$.

\emph{Search.} Forward search from $(c_0,P_0,\mathit{init})$: actions apply
the STRIPS effect to $P$ and conjoin assignment/nondeterminism constraints onto
$\phi$ at fresh SSA indices; guards and goals are satisfiability queries
$\phi\wedge\psi$ in $\mathcal L$, decidable by assumption; choices branch.

\emph{Widening at back edges.} On traversing a back edge to loop head $X$
with predicate state $P$, the procedure prunes if the pair $(X,P)$ has been
recorded at a back edge before; otherwise it records $(X,P)$, \emph{havocs}
the numeric summary --- replaces $\phi$ by $\top$ and advances every SSA
index --- and re-enters the loop body.

\emph{Termination.} Between back edges the remaining step structure is a
finite tree, so only finitely many queries occur. Each back-edge traversal
either records a new pair $(X,P)$ or prunes; there are at most
$|C|\cdot 2^{|\Pred|}$ such pairs, so the search terminates.

\emph{Soundness for refutation.} Havoc widening only enlarges the denoted
world set ($\top$ is entailed by any $\phi$), so the widened system
over-approximates the concrete one; by Theorem~\ref{thm:tol} a refutation on
it transfers. Pruning is exact for the widened system: after widening, a
configuration is fully determined by $(X,P)$ --- the constraint is always
$\top$ and the continuation at a loop head is fixed by the (unique) binder ---
so a pruned re-visit explores exactly the state space already explored, and no
goal-satisfying run is lost.

\emph{Definiteness.} Within the fragment the procedure always exits with a
witness or an exhausted search, never \textsc{Unknown}; hence achievability is
decidable. The price of the havoc is possible spurious witnesses \emph{through}
loops (a numeric refinement re-established each iteration is forgotten), which
lands on the incomplete side of Proposition~\ref{prop:inc}, never the sound
side.
\end{proof}
\begin{theorem}[Undecidability under autonomy]\label{thm:undec}
If skills may spawn participants at run time (unbounded $\mathcal R$) with dynamic
channels, achievability is undecidable.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By reduction from control-state reachability of communicating finite-state
machines (CFSMs), undecidable already for two machines connected by unbounded
FIFO channels~\cite{bz}. Given a CFSM system $(M_1,M_2)$ and a target control
state $q^\ast$ of $M_1$, construct a skill system as follows. Each machine
becomes a role whose local control states are encoded in branch structure; each
FIFO channel is simulated by a stream of spawned single-message courier
participants: to send letter $m$, a role spawns a fresh courier and hands it
$m$; couriers deliver in spawn order to the receiving role (dynamic channels
give each courier its own session with the receiver, and an integer counter
--- expressible in $\mathcal L$ --- sequences delivery, so FIFO order is
preserved). Transitions of $M_i$ on sending/receiving $m$ become the
corresponding spawn/receive steps; the world is used only for one marker
predicate $\mathsf{hit}$, added by a capability that $M_1$'s encoding invokes
exactly upon entering $q^\ast$; the goal is $\gphi=\mathsf{hit}$. The
construction is computable, and runs of the skill system correspond
bijectively to runs of the CFSM system, with $\mathsf{hit}$ reachable iff
$q^\ast$ is. A decision procedure for achievability in the unbounded-spawn
fragment would therefore decide CFSM control-state reachability. Hence none
exists, and a sound checker must degrade: refute what is refutable without
bounding the topology (\S\ref{sec:closure}), and answer \textsc{Unknown}
otherwise.
\end{proof}

\noindent The two theorems draw the line, and the line is autonomy itself. A
session type's hard case is \emph{non-local choice}; an autonomous agent's defining
behaviour is to make such choices and spawn helpers on the fly. The feature that
makes agents agentic is exactly the feature that crosses from decidable into
undecidable.

\subsection{The establisher closure: refutation that survives autonomy}
\label{sec:closure}
Theorem~\ref{thm:undec} bounds \emph{reachability analysis}, not every
refutation. Some verdicts quantify over all protocols at once and therefore do
not care whether the topology is static.

\begin{lemma}[Establisher closure]\label{lem:closure}
Let $\mathcal E(\Gamma,\mathit{init}) = \mathit{init} \cup
\bigcup_{a\in\dom(\Gamma)}\Add_a$. For every protocol $G$ over $\Gamma$ ---
of any shape, including protocols that spawn unboundedly many participants, so
long as every participant acts through $\Gamma$ --- and every reachable
configuration $(G',W)$ with $W=\langle B,N\rangle$:
$\ \{p : B(p)=\mathrm{tt}\}\subseteq\mathcal E(\Gamma,\mathit{init})$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Induction on $\reachs$. Initially $B_0=\mathit{init}\subseteq\mathcal E$.
\textsc{R-Msg}, \textsc{R-Sel}, \textsc{R-Rec} (and participant spawning)
leave $W$ untouched. For \textsc{R-Act} through $a$,
$B'=(B\cup\Add_a)\setminus\Del_a\subseteq B\cup\Add_a
\subseteq\mathcal E\cup\Add_a=\mathcal E$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Protocol-independent refutation]\label{cor:gamma}
Let $\widehat{\gphi}$ be $\gphi$ with every predicate outside
$\mathcal E(\Gamma,\mathit{init})$ replaced by $\bot$, every remaining
predicate by a fresh free boolean, and every arithmetic comparison by a fresh
free boolean. If $\widehat{\gphi}$ is unsatisfiable, then no run of \emph{any}
protocol over $\Gamma$ satisfies $\gphi$ --- in particular the verdict is
sound outside the decidable fragment of Theorem~\ref{thm:dec}.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
Suppose some reachable $W$ satisfied $\gphi$. By Lemma~\ref{lem:closure}
every predicate outside $\mathcal E$ is false in $W$, matching the $\bot$
substitution; valuing each remaining free boolean by its truth in $W$ yields a
model of $\widehat{\gphi}$, contradicting unsatisfiability.
\end{proof}

The checker decides Corollary~\ref{cor:gamma} with one SMT query before
anything topology-dependent, so a skill that spawns helpers \emph{and} pursues
a goal conjunct nothing in $\Gamma$ establishes is still refuted --- with the
dead conjuncts as the certificate --- rather than surrendered to
\textsc{Unknown}. This generalizes the \id{FlightInstance} argument of the
mechanization from one spec to a decision rule. It also sharpens the vocabulary
of verdicts: \textsc{Goal\_Unsat} via the closure refutes \emph{every} plan
over these tools, while \textsc{Goal\_Unsat} from the search refutes
\emph{this} plan; the two carry different repair advice (acquire a tool
vs.\ fix the plan).

\section{Strengthening the Discipline}
Compiling real, deployed skills (\S\ref{sec:realeval}) exposed three places
where the discipline as stated either refuted skills that are perfectly
executable or answered less than it could. Each fix is a small extension with
its own soundness argument; none touches the trusted core's guarantee.

\subsection{Observed choice: coordination inside a conversation}
\label{sec:obs}
Compacting real consumer skills, every initial \textsc{Non\_Projectable}
refutation traced to the same modelling mismatch: a choice resolved
\emph{inside a live conversation} (the user picks an option in chat; the
business answers on a phone call), where the classic unobserved-choice
condition does not apply because the medium announces the outcome to every
participant. \textsc{Proj-Obs} (\S4.3) captures this.

\begin{proposition}[Soundness of observed choice]\label{prop:obs}
Let $D(G)$ desugar every
$\mathsf p\triangleright^{\mathrm{obs}}\{\ell_i:G_i\}$ into
$\mathsf p\triangleright\{\ell_i:
\mathsf p{\to}\mathsf q_1{:}\ell_i.\cdots.\,
\mathsf p{\to}\mathsf q_k{:}\ell_i.\,G_i\}$, prefixing each branch with an
explicit broadcast of the selected label to the other roles
$\mathsf q_1,\dots,\mathsf q_k$. Then (i) $D(G)$ is projectable by
\textsc{Proj-Brn} exactly where $G$ is projectable by \textsc{Proj-Obs}, with
identical projections up to the added receive prefixes; and (ii) $D$ preserves
may-reachability, since \textsc{R-Msg} steps do not change the world.
Consequently checking $G$ with \textsc{Proj-Obs} equals checking a standard
global type whose communication the conversation medium performs implicitly.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
(i) Under $D$, every branch of the choice begins, for each non-selector
$\mathsf q$, with the distinguishing receive $\mathsf p?\ell_i$, which is
precisely the premise of \textsc{Proj-Brn}; the resulting local type is
$\&\{\ell_i:T_i\}$ with $T_i$ the projection of the branch body --- the
\textsc{Proj-Obs} result. For the selector both give
$\oplus\{\ell_i:\cdot\}$ over the same continuations. (ii) is immediate
from \textsc{R-Msg}: inserted messages neither guard nor affect $W$, so runs
and goal-satisfaction correspond one-to-one.
\end{proof}

The observation behind the fix is worth stating on its own: \emph{unobserved
choice is a ${\ge}3$-party asynchronous phenomenon}. In a binary synchronous
session the selection is the communication; only when a third party must act
on a branch it never hears about does realizability fail. Compaction should
therefore mark conversation-embedded choices observed, and reserve explicit
\id{msg} steps for genuinely asynchronous handoffs between separate agents.

\subsection{Adversarial achievability: when the environment picks}
The diamond of \S4.2 is \emph{angelic}: every choice, including one resolved
by an external party, is existential. A skill whose goal is reachable only if
the business says yes is angelically achievable --- true, but weaker than an
operator often wants to know. We add a \emph{must} mode. Choices may be
annotated \emph{external}; define $\achbox^{\!\mathrm{adv}}$ by replacing
\textsc{Reach-Or} on external choices with its universal dual:
\begin{mathpar}
\inferrule[Reach-And-Ext]{\forall k\in I.\ \Gamma;(G_k,W)\vDash^{\mathrm{adv}}\Diamond\gphi}
  {\Gamma;(\mathsf p\triangleright^{\mathrm{ext}}\{\ell_i:G_i\},W)\vDash^{\mathrm{adv}}\Diamond\gphi}
\end{mathpar}
so the agent's own choices remain existential while the environment's are
demonic: AND--OR reachability, i.e.\ the existence of a winning strategy for
the agent coalition against the declared model.

\begin{proposition}[Compositional refutation soundness]\label{prop:adv}
An adversarial refutation is sound relative to the declared model: if the
checker reports $\Gamma;G\nvDash^{\mathrm{adv}}\Diamond\gphi$, then for
every agent strategy there is a resolution of external choices under which the
goal is not reached.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
By induction on the search tree. At an external choice the checker refutes by
exhibiting a branch $\ell_k$ whose subtree it refutes; the environment plays
$\ell_k$. At the leaves and along internal structure, refutation of a subtree
is may-refutation, which is sound by Theorem~\ref{thm:ref}: no run of that
subtree reaches the goal at all, in particular none under the chosen strategy.
The induction glues the environment's branch selections into a single
spoiling strategy.
\end{proof}
The asymmetry is preserved but flipped in an instructive way: adversarial
\emph{achievable} inherits the incompleteness of Proposition~\ref{prop:inc}
per branch, while adversarial \emph{refutation} composes may-refutations and
stays trustworthy. The same annotation vocabulary is the natural substrate for
the Layer-B $\Box$ analysis anticipated in \S4.4.

\subsection{Counterexample-guided compaction repair}
\label{sec:cegar}
The trust boundary makes compaction errors \emph{inspectable}; it does not by
itself make them \emph{cheap to fix}. In practice the dominant compaction
artifact is structural (a conversation modelled as an unannounced handoff),
and the checker's refutation names it precisely. We therefore close a bounded
loop at the boundary: when the trusted checker refutes with
\textsc{Non\_Projectable}, the counterexample is returned to the untrusted
compactor with a repair instruction restricted to communication structure ---
it may mark a choice observed or add the informing messages the prose actually
describes, and may \emph{not} add capabilities, alter effects, or weaken the
goal. The candidate re-enters through the same schema gate and checker.
Soundness is untouched --- every verdict is still computed by the trusted core
on the pack in front of it --- and the restriction matters: repairing
\textsc{Missing\_Capability} or \textsc{Goal\_Unsat} this way would invite
the model to invent tools or dilute the goal, exactly what rule~1 of the
compaction contract forbids. On the real-skill corpus one round of repair
resolves every structural artifact (\S\ref{sec:realeval}).

\subsection{Admission control at the boundary}
Orthogonally to the type discipline, the compiler's inputs are \emph{bundles}
--- prose, scripts, manifests, permission metadata --- and honesty of the
declared $\Gamma$ begins with the bundle not being hostile. A deterministic
SkillSpector-style pre-pass~\cite{skillspector} vets manifest consistency,
invisible/bidirectional Unicode, instruction-injection patterns in
descriptions and hidden comments, pipe-to-shell and decode-and-execute
patterns in embedded code, and consistency of prose tool use with declared
\id{allowed-tools}. It is admission control, not typing: it asks whether the
bundle should be trusted enough to compile at all.

\section{Implementation}
\label{sec:impl}
The compiler is a Python package (\id{skillc}) deciding all four premises of
\textsc{Ach}: capability soundness, realizability by actual projection with
merge (\textsc{Proj-Sel}/\textsc{Brn}/\textsc{Mrg}/\textsc{Obs}),
conformance by coinductive Gay--Hole subtyping
(\textsc{Sub-Ext}/\textsc{Int}), and may-reachability over Z3 with the
procedure of Theorem~\ref{thm:dec} --- tail-recursive loops explored with
predicate-state saturation and havoc widening at back edges, dynamic spawning
degrading to \textsc{Unknown} after the closure certificate of
Corollary~\ref{cor:gamma} has been tried. Front-ends produce packs three
ways: a deterministic markdown compactor (frontmatter and prose tool
extraction with line-number provenance, judged against pluggable capability
profiles of the target runtime), an embedded-pack escape hatch, and the
untrusted LLM compactor with the schema gate and the bounded repair loop of
\S\ref{sec:cegar}. The Coq development (Theorems~\ref{thm:ref}--\ref{thm:cap}
plus the concrete instance) checks under Coq 8.18 with no axioms, verified by
\id{Print Assumptions}; it certifies the trusted core's soundness argument,
while the compiler itself is the Python implementation. On success the checker
returns the witness path; on refutation, the failing premise and its frontier
(missing capabilities, unsatisfiable guard, dead goal conjuncts, the
non-projectable role, or the non-conforming skill). The package is
${\sim}2.5$k lines of Python (checker, session engine, gates, front-ends,
audit, CLI); the CLI (\id{skillc compile\,|\,check\,|\,scan\,|\,audit\,|\,eval})
maps verdicts to exit codes (0 achievable, 1 impossible, 2 error, 3 unknown)
so achievability can gate continuous integration for skill repositories, and
\id{skillc eval} reproduces every number in \S\ref{sec:eval}. All solver
queries are resolved conservatively: an SMT \emph{unknown} counts as
satisfiable, so refutations rest only on definite unsats.

\subsection{Real, deployed skills}
\label{sec:realeval}
The proof-of-concept corpus answers ``does the checker match the theory'';
the sharper question is whether the \emph{compiler} validates the goal
achievability of skills people actually run. We evaluate on Anthropic's public
skills corpus (32 \id{SKILL.md} bundles: consumer workflows such as
booking-by-phone and grocery ordering, and document-processing skills), in
three regimes.

\paragraph{Capability level, deterministic.} The deterministic front-end
compacts each skill against a pluggable \emph{capability profile} of the
target runtime. Under the consumer-runtime profile, \textbf{32/32} skills are
achievable --- zero false alarms on deployed skills, the empirical face of
Theorem~\ref{thm:ref}. Under a coding-CLI profile, \textbf{16/32} are refuted
\textsc{Missing\_Capability} with the exact absent tool and source line
(e.g.\ \id{ask\_user\_input\_v0}, a consumer-app widget no CLI grants):
the same skill, two capability contexts, two verdicts --- achievability is a
judgment about $\langle$skill, $\Gamma\rangle$, never the skill alone.
Mutation testing closes the loop in both directions: removing one invoked tool
from the profile flips every affected skill to \textsc{Missing\_Capability}
naming exactly that tool, and granting the named frontier back flips it to
achievable (Theorem~\ref{thm:cap} on real data).

\paragraph{Semantic level, live compaction.} Four representative consumer
skills were compacted by a live LLM into semantic packs (goals such as
\emph{booked $\wedge$ calendar-updated $\wedge$ user-informed} with
per-step guards), through the schema gate and, where needed, one round of the
\S\ref{sec:cegar} repair loop. All four check \textsc{Achievable} --- no
false alarm on deployed skills at the semantic level either.

\paragraph{Seeded semantic faults.} Against each achievable semantic pack we
seed the two canonical wounds with known ground truth: \emph{drop} a
capability the plan invokes, and \emph{strip} a goal conjunct's establishers
from every tool that provides it. The compiler refuted \textbf{6/6} mutants,
naming the dropped tool (\textsc{Missing\_Capability}, frontier) or the dead
conjunct (\textsc{Goal\_Unsat} via Corollary~\ref{cor:gamma}) in every
case. The admission pre-pass, run over the same 32 real bundles, reports zero
error-severity findings, while a planted poisoned bundle (invisible Unicode,
description injection, hidden HTML-comment instruction, pipe-to-shell
install) trips every detector class.

\section{Related Work}
\noindent\textbf{Prior-art note.} The claims below reflect the authors' survey at
submission time; the fusion's novelty should be confirmed against a systematic
search of the planning-meets-behavioural-types literature, which is dispersed
across communities.

\emph{Multiparty session types}~\cite{honda,liprojection} supply projection,
deadlock-freedom, and subtyping~\cite{gayhole}. We reuse this for the realizability
premise but add a world and a goal, which classical session types do not model.
\emph{Automated planning}~\cite{strips} supplies capability-guarded goal
reachability; we reuse its frame semantics but embed it in a multiparty
choreography. The contribution is the \emph{fusion}, decided over an
LLM-synthesized, goal-marked global type. Recent agent-verification work is
adjacent but differently aimed: \emph{provable coordination via message sequence
charts}~\cite{msc} projects a global choreography for LLM agents but targets
coordination correctness, not goal achievability; \emph{VeriGuard}~\cite{veriguard}
separates offline policy verification from online monitoring as a safety shield;
\emph{agent behavioural contracts}~\cite{abc} bring design-by-contract to single
agents.

Skill-bundle scanners and verified-skill catalogs are complementary.
SkillSpector~\cite{skillspector} and the NVIDIA verified-agent-skills
pipeline~\cite{nvskills} address the same deployment object --- portable agent
skills --- but ask a different question: a pre-publication security control
normalizes a bundle and runs deterministic scanners (static pattern detectors,
AST and taint analyzers, manifest-consistency and metadata-poisoning checks,
supply-chain checks). Valuable admission control, but not a compiler or type
discipline: it defines no operational semantics of goal-marked skills,
projects nothing, decides no reachability, proves no refutation soundness. In
our architecture such a pass sits at the compiler's boundary (\S7.4): before
compaction it vets the incoming bundle; after compaction it supports the
honesty of declared capabilities. The type checker then consumes the sanitized
pack and proves the narrower claim that the declared goal is not structurally
impossible. None of the above frames the static check as goal achievability
over a synthesized protocol, nor gives the refutation-sound /
achievement-incomplete asymmetry with a mechanized proof. Finally, the trust boundary connects to learning with verifiable
rewards: the checker's sound \emph{impossible} verdicts are cheap, on-distribution
negative labels --- a reward signal needing no LLM judge --- a loop we leave to
future work.

\section{Limitations and Future Work}
\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{1pt}
\item \textbf{Relative soundness.} Everything is proved about the declared $\Gamma$
and $S$. If the prose lies (a ``read-only'' tool that writes), the checker verifies
a fiction; honest-declaration is a separate obligation, of which this makes only
the interaction slice decidable.
\item \textbf{Static topology for totality.} Dynamic spawning drops the procedure
to a semi-decision (Theorem~\ref{thm:undec}); a practical system answers
\textsc{Unknown} rather than guessing.
\item \textbf{Trusted abstraction.} A coarse $\alpha$ silently widens tolerance
(more false achievable), never breaking Theorem~\ref{thm:ref} but weakening
usefulness.
\item \textbf{Engineering gaps.} The mechanization covers soundness of
abstract reachability (Theorems~\ref{thm:ref}--\ref{thm:cap}); the newer
results --- Lemma~\ref{lem:closure}, Theorem~\ref{thm:dec}'s procedure,
Propositions~\ref{prop:obs} and~\ref{prop:adv} --- are proved on paper here
and their mechanization is routine future work. The merge $\sqcap$ implements
label-union on external branches with structural recursion elsewhere --- a
sound core of, not the complete, merge lattice; havoc widening forgets
\emph{all} numeric state at back edges (always in the tolerant direction).
The real-skill corpus is one vendor's public catalog (32 bundles) and the
semantic-level evaluation covers four of them with one compaction model;
scaling both is next.
\item \textbf{Compaction quality is a first-class variable.} The real-skill
study shows the checker's verdict is only as meaningful as the pack: the two
systematic artifacts we found (under-granted runtime abilities, unannotated
conversational choices) both produce false refutations --- safe-side errors,
surfaced with counterexamples, and fixable by prompt contract and bounded
repair --- but a compaction study across models and prompt contracts is
needed before quantitative claims about the pass direction generalize.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}
An LLM can draft an agent's plan from intent; the open question has been whether
anything can tell, cheaply and before execution, that the plan is doomed. For the
\emph{achievability} question the answer is yes, with a guarantee: fuse planning's
capability-guarded reachability with session types' projectability over a
goal-marked global type, treat the LLM's compaction as untrusted, and prove the
checker sound for refutation. The verdict \emph{impossible} is then never wrong;
\emph{achievable} is honest about the two edges it defers. The same machinery, with
the diamond flipped to a box, extends upward to per-step safety --- a layered route
from ``can this agent even do that?'' to ``will it do it safely?''
